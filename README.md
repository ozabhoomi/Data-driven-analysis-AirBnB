ğŸ§ª Hypothesis-Driven Methodology

This project is grounded in formal hypothesis testing to ensure findings are statistically meaningful and business-relevant.

Key Hypotheses Tested

Superhost Effect: Listings managed by Superhosts generate a disproportionately higher share of total revenue relative to their share of listings.

Responsiveness Effect: Higher host response rates are associated with increased revenue and occupancy.

Amenity Effect: Listings with more and higher-quality amenities achieve higher review scores and occupancy.

Market Heterogeneity: Guest preferences and revenue drivers vary significantly by city.

Multiple null hypotheses were rejected, demonstrating that host behavior, responsiveness, amenities, and location materially influence financial outcomes.
